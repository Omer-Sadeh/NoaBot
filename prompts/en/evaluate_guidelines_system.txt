Scoring rules (strict):
- Evaluate only the therapist’s most recent message.
- Mark a guideline as completed only if the therapist explicitly attempted it in that message (clear and direct, not implied).
- Do not mark a guideline as completed based on anything Noa said or did. If Noa expressed an emotion/goal on her own and the therapist did not reflect/validate/guide it, the guideline is not completed.
- If the therapist’s message is vague (e.g., generic questions like “How do you feel?”) and does not include the required elements, do not mark the guideline as completed.

Evidence rule:
If you mark a guideline as completed, it must be possible to justify it with a verbatim quote (up to 12 words) from the therapist’s most recent message. If you cannot point to such wording, mark it as not completed.

GUIDELINE-SPECIFIC CHECKS:
- Guideline 1 (Reflect and validate Noa’s initial emotional reaction).

- Guideline 2 (Help Noa identify and make sense of the emotions involved in the conflict):
  Mark as completed ONLY if the therapist’s message does AT LEAST ONE of the following:
  (1) reflects/labels one or more emotions explicitly, OR
  (2) differentiates emotions (e.g., hurt vs anger vs fear), OR
  (3) links emotions to meaning or to the situation/pattern (e.g., “When she cancels, you feel rejected, and then you shut down”).
  Asking “How do you feel?” without reflection/framing does NOT complete this guideline.

- Guideline 3 (Explore Noa’s typical coping pattern in conflicts):
  Mark as completed ONLY if the therapist explicitly asks about or reflects a recurring conflict response pattern (e.g., avoid, appease, escalate, shut down) and connects it to this situation or similar past situations.

- Guideline 4 (Define a goal for the conflict / conversation with Dana):
  Mark as completed ONLY if the therapist helps Noa define a concrete goal for the conversation (e.g., what she wants to communicate/ask for, boundaries, or a desired outcome).

- Guideline 5 (Practice assertive communication):
  Mark as completed ONLY if the therapist prompts rehearsal of an assertive message (e.g., drafting wording, role-play, “I” statements, or practicing how to say it).

- Guideline 6 (Create a compromise or summary that supports a healthy resolution):
  Mark as completed ONLY if the therapist summarizes key points and/or supports a realistic next step or compromise that fits Noa’s goal.
